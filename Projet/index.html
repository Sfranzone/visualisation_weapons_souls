Daggers:
Daggers are light and easily transportable weapons. In games, this is reflected in the equipment weight and attack speed. They also have a higher critical hit rate than average to compensate for their relatively low base damage.
0%
Straight Swords:
Straight swords are weapons that can be wielded with one hand. They are balanced for combat with both offensive and defensive potential. Essentially, straight swords form the basis of balanced combat.
0%
Greatswords:
Unlike classic swords, greatswords focus on powerful attacks, their range, and also provide decent defense if necessary. However, to balance these advantages, greatswords are heavy in video games, which is not the case in reality.
0%
Colossal Swords:
The bigger, the more painful it is. That's the whole principle of colossal swords. Perfect for piercing through your enemies, or even crushing them, at the expense of having to carry a high weight.
0%
Thrusting Swords:
Swords are not only used for cutting, but can also be used for thrusting while maintaining distance. While all swords are capable of this, thrusting swords allow for quick thrust attacks to strike while having the opportunity to quickly retreat due to their lightweight.
0%
Heavy Thrusting Swords:
Although this is not very common in reality, games like to bring all types of weapons to another dimension. With heavy thrusting swords, you maintain a logic of thrusting attack like their normal-sized version, but with increased attack power at the expense of maneuverability.
0%
Curved Swords:
Curved swords are practical for slashing your opponent. Unlike many other sword models, the blade isn't pointed, allowing this type of sword to attack from multiple angles.
0%
Curved Greatswords:
Despite their considerable weight, these weapons allow for strikes that can lacerate their victim. Unlike smaller versions, this model deals more damage but is less maneuverable, leaving a greater opportunity to miss the target.
0%
Katanas:
This is a traditional Japanese sword. Slightly curved, it allows for swift attacks when drawn from its sheath. Useful for close combat, however, it does not allow thrust attacks like Western models. With growing fascination in Japan, thanks in part to anime and manga, this weapon is widely used in video games (and often a good choice).
0%
Twinblades:
Sometimes two are better than one. This is the case with twin blades. Instead of having a sword pointed in one direction, this weapon is a double sword oriented forward and backward. This allows its user more opportunities for attacks and defenses from various sides. Surprisingly, this weapon is quite light and therefore maneuverable.
0%
Axes:
Less versatile than a sword, axes allow for powerful attacks quickly, at the expense of low defense.
0%
Greataxes:
Heavier, sturdier, and even more violent than their normal-sized cousin, the greataxe delivers even more powerful blows than a standard axe.
0%
Hammers:
Unlike many weapons, the hammer is not there to slice, shear, or cut, but to purely crush and grind its unfortunate opponent to the bone.
0%
Flails:
Similar to the hammer, the flail has the property of crushing its opponent, but is also designed to destroy armor in order to remove any protection from its adversary.
0%
Great Hammers:
In case a regular hammer seemed too small for you, here's the solution to your problems: a great hammer. Heavy and unwieldy, you can be sure that if you hit your target, it will turn into jelly.
0%
Colossal Weapons:
This category, which could have included other oversized weapons such as great hammers and colossal swords, has been set apart because it lists even different weapons, such as scepters used as maces. The logic remains the same: to crush.
0%
Spears:
To keep one's opponent at a distance while still being in contact with them, the spear is an ideal weapon. Primarily used for thrusting at one's opponent, it can also be used for striking and countering attacks. Nevertheless, in reality, with a spear that has a wooden handle, the risk of taking a blow is too high, and keeping one's distance is a better alternative.
0%
Great Spears:
More massive than a traditional spear, great spears allow you to more easily keep your target at bay, albeit with more restricted mobility.
0%
Halberds:
By combining a spear, a pike, a harpoon, and an axe, we get the halberd. In games, they are primarily used for their dual potential between striking and thrusting while maintaining distance. In reality, they were also used for hooking a person, unhorsing a rider, and using the pike opposite the axe as an alternative against an attack from a different angle.
0%
Reapers:
The scythe is literally used for reaping, but compared to a curved sword, the scythe allows you to do so from a greater distance, albeit with a slower movement.
0%
Whips:
The whip isn't as practical as in Indiana Jones movies. It can be used to strike from a distance, injuring the enemy and causing them pain. While this is easily lethal in games, in our world, it takes a good number of strikes to kill a person with this object. However, using it to catch objects or disarm someone by whipping their hand is possible in reality. Unfortunately, this option is not available in souls-like games.

Kusarigama:
Only available in Nioh (for our selection of games), the Kusarigama is like a pickaxe for planting, but also for cutting like a scythe. The size of an axe, the handle of the Kusarigama can be attached to a rope or chain to throw it and reach more distant targets.
0%
Fists:
Sometimes the classic method is worth it. Fast and allowing for close control of one's opponent, close combat weapons like spiked gloves allowing for punching like a boxing glove, or even tonfas, a type of Japanese baton.
0%
Claws:
Claws are very short-range weapons but are very fast, allowing for agile attacks against opponents.
0%
Light Bows:
Bows allow the use of arrows, providing great distance from the target. Light bows maintain this ability, albeit with a projectile less powerful than a larger bow, but benefit from great mobility and a satisfactory firing speed.
0%
Bows:
The bow is the quintessential ranged weapon of the Middle Ages. In movies and video games, the rate of fire is much higher than that of a real archer, adding more dynamism. This does not detract from its advantage as a long-range weapon.
0%
Greatbows:
Greatbows are massive bows that launch heavy projectiles. Not very maneuverable, their power compensates for their size issue. Like colossal swords, this type of bow is not a common thing in reality.
0%
Crossbows:
The crossbow follows the same logic as a bow, but it fires a "bolt" (a small arrow designed for crossbows). Relatively light and maneuverable, this weapon provides great accuracy (for its time) in shooting. Its reload time is longer than that of a bow because of the cranks needed to place the string in the firing position.
0%
Ballistas:
Ballistas and cannons exist, but they are not commonly used as personal weapons. The shots are powerful, but the bulkiness is so great that it's not really worth it.
0%
Magical casting sticks:
Wands and other magical staffs are useless for hitting, but they cast powerful spells that can disintegrate anyone... as long as the magical power and the spell in question are available.
0%
Sacred seals:
Sacred seals, only appearing in Elden Ring, are a variation of item for casting spells, giving greater power to spells linked to the grace of the World Tree.
0%
Tools (offensive):
Every adventurer must have tools with them. Torches, grenades, darts, and many items are used to create opportunities to attack or to find ourselves in the middle of where we are.
0%
Handguns:
Handguns are ranged weapons. In our selection, only Remnant II has this weapon. Mostly for short distances, these weapons serve more as secondary weapons than primary weapons, as support weapons when the main weapon needs to be reloaded but the enemy does not give you the time.
0%
Long Guns (all kinds of rifles like):
Long guns and other weapons with a long barrel are weapons that can excel at long distances. We specify "can" because, like the pistols in Remnant II, there is a variety of these weapons, mainly used for medium and long distances, but they are also powerful at close range, and in some cases, this is their specialty, such as heavy machine guns.
0%
Trick Weapons:
Exclusive to the Bloodborne universe, trick weapons are melee and/or ranged weapons that change their usage depending on how they are transformed.

Now that you had fun with the images full visualization, it is time for some reading. Here we will give you some more straight forwards datas, but we will explain them to you.
It will give a better understanding of what those datas allowed us to discover about those weapon categories.

Bar plot

This bar plot aligns all games at once to check which games have the most of each weapon type. By checking the different categories, we discovered each sort of game has a type of weapon that prevails. The dark medieval like Elden Ring and Dark Souls III own the biggest amount of "great" weapons. Oriental games such as Sekiro and Nioh possess the biggest number of Asian weapon types, like the Katanas, Kusarigamas and Spears. Remnant II, being a multiverse game based on the modern era, is the only one where players can enjoy shooting with handguns. Lastly, Bloodborne, placed in nightmares from the Victorian Era, they got their own category of "trick weapons" (those are modified weapons from this era. It can include musket like, swords, scythe, and so on), which is the largest part of the arsenal you can get in this game.
Force graph
Display the Force Chart
By clicking the link above, you have access to our graph. We display it on another page, so you can move it freely (moving the graph around and each nodes to see the different connexions).

With this force graph, we can directly see the influence of the two games who own the most weapons and the biggest diversity: Elden Ring and Dark Souls III. Each one is on one side of the main group of weapons, holding all their links. In the opposite, we see one node being isolated from the others: Sekiro. Indeed, this game is more focus on the story and the missions, while the others have more storytelling with the small chats you can have with NPCs. This is why this game has the lowest number of weapons.

One game that displays stats than it should is Bloodborne. With only 26 weapons (when Elden Ring has 313), it owns a decent amount because of its variety of choices. The trick weapons, Bloodborne's weapons signature, are at the count of eight. The rest is dissipated between 11 categories. This FromSoftware game, while having a decent playtime, is still short enough to not obtain from the devs a large panel for each type, like Dark Souls did.

For the weapon types, we see 10 out of the 35 groups have a small influence overall. This is explained with their low density of games linked to them. Those are one or two at most. We also note, in correlation with the other graphs, it doesn't mean those nodes have a low count. By looking at the weight of their links, we clearly observe, for example, the handguns and the Kusarigamas are heavy (respectively 21 and 28 units).

Pie chart

With the observation of this pie plot, we realized the quantity of each game is not what we thought. When we looked at the force graph, Remnant II seemed to have a greater choice than Nioh, diversity-wise, it is a fact, but general wise, it is false. Remnant has 19 types for a total of 94 weapons, while Nioh has 9 for 216. Katanas is the more populated in Nioh, with 70 instances.
